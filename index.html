About Me

Operator by training.
Builder by nature.

I've spent my whole life betting on myself. I joined the Army at 18 because I saw it clearly: short-term pain, long-term gain. It paid for my degree at Michigan Ross. Ross got me into Accenture. Accenture taught me that you can walk into any organization, develop a strong plan, and deliver. That instinct has driven everything since.

I work the way I do because I've never been afraid not to know something. I ask questions because I'd rather look uninformed for thirty seconds than make the wrong call for three months. In practice, that means I build shared understanding fast. I run tight, well-scoped projects with clear expectations. I push for root causes over symptoms. I deliver organized, high-value work as a result.

Outside of work, I'm a Detroiter through and through: president of my neighborhood watch, member of the Ford/Dearborn Astronomy Club, landlord and hands-on renovator, and one of the most social people you'll meet. I've lived in three countries, visited fourteen, and I'd still pick Detroit every time. I live here with my partner Sophie, our dog Daisy, and our cat Cait.
